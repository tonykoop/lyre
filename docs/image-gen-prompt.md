# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A small, bench-buildable plucked lyre presented as a finished physical object: a compact open frame of solid hardwood with two upward-curving arms rising from the body and meeting a horizontal crossbar (yoke) across the top, forming the lyre's classic recognizable U-shaped silhouette. A flat thin wooden soundboard of pale spruce or cedar (or light birch ply) closes the lower body, with ten monofilament strings fanning from individual zither tuning pins seated in a row along the crossbar down to a low bridge resting on the soundboard, passing over a slender nut. The strings are pale nylon or fluorocarbon, near-translucent, in a clean ten-string set. Materials read as warm oiled hardwood frame with continuous flowing grain through the arms and a lighter satin-finished soundboard; hardware is small steel zither pins with squared tuning-key tops. Hand-held tabletop scale, roughly forearm-length tall. Clean neutral light-gray studio background, soft directional studio light from upper left, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same ten-string hardwood lyre staged upright on a maker's workbench in a small woodworking shop, leaning gently against a low wooden block, with curls of wood shavings, a few hand tools, and a tuning key resting nearby. Warm natural window light rakes across the soundboard and catches the pale fanned strings and the flowing grain of the arms. Shallow depth of field keeps the frame and string fan crisp while the shop softens into bokeh behind it. Environmental storytelling of a prototype just brought up to pitch; concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the lyre's signature crossbar and pin row: the horizontal hardwood yoke spanning between the two arms, fitted with a row of small steel zither tuning pins, each wound with a pale monofilament string that drops away in a fan toward the bridge. Show the warm oiled grain of the yoke running continuously into the arm joints, the squared tops of the pins, the slight catch of string wraps, and the smooth contact where strings cross the nut. Raking side light emphasizes wood texture, metal sheen, and the taut individual strings, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text/logos/watermarks/fake maker marks, no dimension callouts, no impossible mechanisms, no extra or missing strings beyond a believable ten-string set; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
