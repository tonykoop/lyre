# Epitaph of Seikilos

**Provenance:** Oldest complete surviving musical composition with both melody
and lyrics. Inscribed on a marble stele found in Tralles (modern Aydın, Turkey),
dated c. 1st–2nd century CE. Absolutely public domain.

**Range:** D4–C5. Fits the lyre comfortably in A Mixolydian (A B C D E F G).

**The inscription reads (translation):**
*"While you live, shine / Have no grief at all / Life exists only for a short while /
And Time demands his due."*

**Practice tips:**
- This piece has no strict meter in the original; the 4/4 bar lines here are a
  modern editorial guide only. Feel free to breathe freely between phrases.
- The G natural (not G♯) is what makes it Mixolydian — enjoy the slightly
  archaic sound.
- Play at a moderate, song-like pace. Imagine you are inscribing the melody
  in stone.
