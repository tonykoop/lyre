# Sakura (Cherry Blossoms)

**Provenance:** Traditional Japanese folk melody, Edo period (1603–1868).
First notarized in Kinkyoku-shū, 1888. Public domain.

**Range:** E4–F5. The In scale used here (A B♭ D E F) is approximated with
Western notation — the B in bar 1 is a passing tone kept natural for lyre
convenience.

**Practice tips:**
- The melody moves in pairs of repeated notes — lean into this two-step feel.
- The repeated-note opening phrase (A A B d) is the heart of the tune; play
  the first note slightly shorter than the second for an anticipatory effect.
- This is marked intermediate because the leap from E to F in bar 3 (e to f)
  crosses strings in a non-intuitive way on lyre — practice that bar alone first.
